## Simultaneous Reconstruction

- Accelerated electron beams interacting with a thin sample acquire phase shifts:  

$$
\begin{aligned}
	\phi\left(\boldsymbol{r}_{\perp}\right) &= \phi_e\left(\boldsymbol{r}_{\perp}\right) + \phi_m \left(\boldsymbol{r}_{\perp}\right) \\
	&= \frac{e}{\hbar v} \int_L V(\boldsymbol{r}_{\perp},z) dz - \frac{e}{\hbar} \int_L \boldsymbol{A}(\boldsymbol{r}_{\perp},z) \cdot d \boldsymbol{r}
\end{aligned}
$$

<Tabs>
<TabItem value="naive" label="'Naive' Subtraction" default>

- Electrostatic contribution is invariant under time-reversal symmetry, while magnetic contribution changes sign
  - For inversion-symmetric materials, we can separate these signals by "flipping" the sample

$$
	\phi_e = \frac{1}{2}\left(\phi^{\rightarrow} + \phi^{\leftarrow} \right) \qquad \text{and} \qquad
	\phi_m = \frac{2}{2}\left(\phi^{\rightarrow} - \phi^{\leftarrow} \right)
$$
</TabItem>

<TabItem value="simultaneous" label="Simultaneous Adjoint Operator">

- Since we know how the forward-model for our measurement changes when we "flip" the sample

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \psi_j^{\rightarrow} &= \mathcal{P} \times \exp(i V) \times \exp(i A_z)  \\
  \psi_j^{\leftarrow} &= \mathcal{P} \times \exp(i V) \times \exp(-i A_z)
\end{aligned}
$$
  
- We can reconstruct the electrostatic and magnetic contributions jointly using an appropriate adjoint:

$$
\begin{aligned}
  V' &= V + \beta \times \left(\Re\left\{ -i \exp(-i (V{\color{red} +}A_z)) \times \mathcal{P}^* \times \Delta \psi_j^{\rightarrow} \right\} {\color{red} +} \Re\left\{ -i \exp(-i (V{\color{red} -}A_z)) \times \mathcal{P}^* \times \Delta \psi_j^{\leftarrow} \right\} \right) /\left|\mathcal{P}\times\exp(i A_z)\right|^2 \\
  A_z' &= A_z + \beta \times \left(\Re\left\{ -i \exp(-i (V{\color{red} +}A_z)) \times \mathcal{P}^* \times \Delta \psi_j^{\rightarrow} \right\} {\color{red} -} \Re\left\{-i \exp(-i (V{\color{red} -}A_z)) \times \mathcal{P}^* \times \Delta \psi_j^{\leftarrow} \right\} \right) /\left|\mathcal{P}\times\exp(i V)\right|^2
\end{aligned}
$$
</TabItem>
