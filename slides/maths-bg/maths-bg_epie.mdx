## Electron Ptychography

- Each diffraction intensity measurement &rarr; non-convex set
  - Problem statement: reconstruct probe $\hat{\mathcal{P}}$ and object $\hat{\mathcal{O}}$ from intensity measurements $I$

$$
\begin{aligned}
  \psi_j(\boldsymbol{r}) &= \hat{\mathcal{P}}(\boldsymbol{r}) \times \hat{\mathcal{O}}(\boldsymbol{r}-\boldsymbol{R}_j) \\
  I_j(\boldsymbol{k}) &= \left| \mathcal{F}\left[\psi_j(\boldsymbol{r})\right]\right|^2
\end{aligned}
$$

- Rich literature of reconstruction algorithms including stochastic gradient descent (ePIE) and proximal-gradient methods:
  - "Fourier" projection
    - Ensures the reconstructed probe and object accurately reproduces the measured intensities
  - "Overlap" projection
    - Ensures the reconstructed probe and object are self-consistent with adjacent scanning positions
